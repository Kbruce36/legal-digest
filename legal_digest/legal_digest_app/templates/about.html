About Our Mission

Bridging the gap between artificial intelligence innovation and fundamental human rights protection through research, documentation, and advocacy.

Our Mission

To safeguard human dignity in the digital age by monitoring, documenting, and challenging the misuse of artificial intelligence. We believe that technology must serve humanity—not undermine the rights and freedoms that define us.

Our Vision

A world where artificial intelligence systems are designed, deployed, and governed with respect for human rights at their core—where innovation and ethics advance together, ensuring technology empowers rather than endangers.

What We Do

Track AI Impacts

We monitor how AI systems affect human rights globally—from facial recognition to algorithmic decision-making in justice, employment, and social services.

Publish Case Summaries

Our team researches and publishes detailed case summaries documenting real-world instances where AI has impacted human rights—both positively and negatively.

Legal Commentary

Expert analysis of emerging AI regulations, court decisions, and policy frameworks from leading human rights lawyers and technology ethicists.

Educational Resources

Toolkits, guides, and research papers to help activists, policymakers, journalists, and citizens understand AI's impact on fundamental rights.

Why It Matters

Artificial intelligence is reshaping societies at an unprecedented pace. Without vigilant oversight, AI systems can entrench discrimination, enable mass surveillance, and erode the freedoms we hold dear.

From predictive policing algorithms that disproportionately target marginalized communities to automated welfare systems that deny benefits without explanation—the stakes are real and urgent. Human rights frameworks, developed over decades, must now be applied to technologies that their drafters never imagined.

The AI and Human Rights Hub exists to ensure that as AI advances, human rights don't retreat. We provide the documentation, analysis, and advocacy needed to hold governments and corporations accountable.
